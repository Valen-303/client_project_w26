Networking & Connections

Building Meaningful Professional Relationships

In the information field, building a strong professional network is as essential as mastering technical skills. By engaging with alumni, industry experts, and mentors, you can gain valuable career insights and uncover hidden job opportunities. We encourage students to actively utilize LinkedIn and the various social platforms provided by UMSI to stay connected.

Networking Tips for UMSI Students

Prepare Your Elevator Pitch

Craft a concise 30-second introduction that highlights who you are, what you study, and what kind of opportunities you are seeking.

Follow Up Promptly

After meeting someone new, send a follow-up message within 24 hours. Mention something specific from your conversation to make it memorable.

Optimize Your LinkedIn

Use a professional photo, write a compelling headline, and keep your profile updated with your latest projects and skills.

Attend CDO Events

Career salons, info sessions, and recruitment fairs are excellent opportunities to meet employers and practice your networking skills.

Networking Dos and Don'ts

Remember: Networking is about building genuine relationships, not just collecting business cards. Focus on how you can add value to others, and the connections will naturally follow.

Do: Be Genuine

Show authentic interest in the other person's work and experiences. Ask thoughtful questions rather than just talking about yourself.

Do: Give Before You Ask

Share an interesting article, offer to help with a project, or make an introduction before asking for favors.

Don't: Be Pushy

Avoid immediately asking for a job or referral. Build rapport first, and let the relationship develop naturally.

Don't: Neglect Your Network

Stay in touch with your contacts regularly, not just when you need something. A quick message or comment goes a long way.
