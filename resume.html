Resume Support

Craft a Resume that Tells Your Story

Your resume is often your first impression—an opportunity to highlight your unique strengths, skills, and experiences to potential employers. Whether you are starting from scratch or looking to polish an existing resume, these resources are here to help you every step of the way.

UMSI students are in high demand, and a clear, well-organized resume will help you stand out among applicants. On this page, you will find practical tips, templates, and guides—all tailored to the needs of School of Information students and aligned with employer expectations.

Need feedback? You can always meet with a CDO Career Coach to review your materials.

Resume Writing Made Easy: Steps to Success

  1. Download a UMSI-Approved Template: Start with a format that works—no need to design from scratch.
  2. Gather Your Experiences: List jobs, internships, volunteer roles, coursework, and projects most relevant to your goals.
  3. Write Impactful Bullets: Use action verbs and highlight your skills, results, and unique contributions.
  4. Tailor for Each Opportunity: Customize your resume for every job or internship you pursue.
  5. Review and Edit: Proofread for errors, check formatting, and ask for feedback from peers or a career coach.

Resume Dos and Don'ts

Do: Use Action Verbs

Start every bullet point with a strong action verb like "designed," "analyzed," "managed," or "developed."

Do: Quantify Results

Include numbers and metrics whenever possible: "Increased engagement by 30%" is stronger than "Improved engagement."

Don't: Include Everything

Be selective. Focus on experiences most relevant to the role you are applying for rather than listing every job.

Don't: Use Generic Objectives

Skip vague objective statements. Instead, let your skills and experiences speak for themselves.

Resume FAQ

How long should my resume be?

For most UMSI students and new grads, one page is best. More experienced professionals may require two pages.

Do I need a different resume for each application?

It is wise to tailor your resume to the requirements and keywords of each opportunity.

Is it okay to include coursework or student projects?

Absolutely! UMSI coursework and projects are valued by employers, especially if they show relevant skills and impact.

How often should I update my resume?

Update your resume every semester, after major projects, or whenever you gain a new role or responsibility.
